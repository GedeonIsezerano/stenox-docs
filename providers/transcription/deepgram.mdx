## Getting Your Free API Key

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit DeepGram Console">
    Go to [console.deepgram.com](https://console.deepgram.com) in your browser.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Sign up for free">
    Click **Sign Up** and create an account with:
    - Email address
    - Password
    - Or sign in with Google/GitHub

    **No credit card required!**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify your email">
    Check your email inbox for a verification link from DeepGram and click it to confirm your account.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Navigate to API Keys">
    Once logged in:
    1. Click on your project name (usually "Default Project")
    2. Go to the **API Keys** section in the left sidebar
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new API key">
    1. Click **Create New Key**
    2. Give it a name (e.g., "Stenox")
    3. Copy the API key - you won't be able to see it again!
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add key to Stenox">
    1. Open Stenox Settings
    2. Go to **Transcription** tab
    3. Select **DeepGram** as provider
    4. Paste your API key
    5. Choose your model (Nova-3 recommended)
    6. Click **Save**
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  **Keep your API key secure!** Treat it like a password. Don't share it publicly or commit it to version control.
</Note>

## Available Models

DeepGram offers several model variants:

### Nova-3 (Recommended)

**Latest and best model:**
- State-of-the-art accuracy
- Fastest processing (< 1 second)
- Best for: All use cases, default choice

**Variants:**
- `nova-3` - Standard, multilingual
- `nova-3-english` - Optimized for English

### Nova-2

**Previous generation:**
- Still excellent accuracy
- Slightly cheaper than Nova-3
- Best for: Budget-conscious users

**Variants:**
- `nova-2` - Standard, multilingual
- `nova-2-english` - Optimized for English

### Flux

**Fast, cost-effective option:**
- Good accuracy for general use
- Cheaper than Nova models
- Best for: High-volume, non-critical transcription

<Tip>
  **Recommendation:** Start with **Nova-3** for the best experience. Your $200 in credits will last a very long time.
</Tip>

## Features

### Streaming Transcription

DeepGram uses WebSocket streaming for real-time results:

- Audio is sent as you speak
- Transcription begins immediately
- Results return in < 1 second after you stop speaking

### Automatic Punctuation

DeepGram automatically adds punctuation:

- Periods, commas, question marks
- Capitalization of proper nouns
- Natural sentence structure

No need to say "comma" or "period" - it's added automatically based on context.

### Speaker Adaptation

DeepGram adapts to your voice:

- Learns your speaking patterns
- Improves accuracy over time
- Better recognition of frequently used terms

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="API key invalid or not working" icon="key">
    - Ensure you copied the entire key (no spaces or extra characters)
    - Check that the key is active in DeepGram console
    - Try creating a new API key
    - Verify your DeepGram account is verified (check email)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Transcription fails or times out" icon="clock">
    - Check your internet connection
    - Verify you have remaining credits in DeepGram console
    - Try a different model (Nova-2 or Flux)
    - Check Stenox logs for error messages
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Poor accuracy or wrong words" icon="spell-check">
    - Ensure you're using Nova-3 (best accuracy)
    - Check language setting matches your speech
    - Reduce background noise
    - Speak more clearly or use a better microphone
    - Add custom vocabulary in Stenox settings
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Running out of credits quickly" icon="credit-card">
    - Check usage in DeepGram console (Billing ‚Üí Usage)
    - Switch to Flux model for cheaper transcription
    - Use WhisperKit (local) when privacy isn't critical to preserve credits
    - Create profiles to automatically switch based on use case
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
